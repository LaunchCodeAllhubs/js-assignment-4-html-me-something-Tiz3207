The land of Origins: Ethiopia

Ethiopia is one of the oldest country in the world and the only sub-Saharan African country that was never colonised by European countries, maintaining its independence throughout the Scramble for Africa. The Ethiopian calendar is unique and has 13 months which makes the Ethiopian calendar 7 years behind the rest of the world. Ethiopia is known for its rich history and culture, including ancient civilizations, unique cuisine and takes first place for the African country with the most UNESCO World Heritage sites. There are 9 Ethiopian Heritage registered by UNESCO. These are Axum, Fasil Ghebbi, Harar Jugol, Awash river, Rock-Hewn monolithic churches of Lalibela, Tiya Stelae Field, Simien Mountains National park, Konso Cultural Landscape and Lower valey of the Omo River.

Axum

Axum is located in northern Ethiopia at Tigray region. It was founded around 400 BCE. Axum symbolizes the importance of the civilization of the ancient Aksumite kingdom. There are several Stelae in Axum. The largest one is Obelisk of Axum and it was built in 4th century by King Ezana The height of the length is 24 metre and weighing 160 tonnes. The Obelisk was taken from its home by Itallian soldiers and returned back after 68 years.

Rock-Hewn monolithic churches of Lalibela

Rock-Hewn monolithic churches of Lalibela is located in the Western Ethiopia highlands. It's a huge religious center for Ethiopian orthodox people. There are thousands of people around the world visit the churches every year. Lalibela is made up of 11 medieval monolithic cave churches. Unlike from all other churches in the world, Lalibela churches are built from the top down. It took 24 years to build all the 11 rock hewn churches. These churches are Biete Giorgis, Biete Denagel, Biete Golgotha Mikael, Biete Maryam, Biete Medhanealem, Biete Meskel, Biete Abba Libanos, Biete Amanuel, Biete Gabriel Rufael, Biete Lehem and Biete Qeddus Mercoreus.

Fasil Castle

Gondar Castle, also known as Fasil Ghebbi, holds a rich historical legacy and stands as a symbol of Ethiopia's medieval power and cultural heritage. Located in the city of Gondar, the castle complex was established in the 17th century during the reign of Emperor Fasilides, who chose the area as his new capital. Gondar Castle became the seat of power for successive Ethiopian emperors, serving as the center of political, religious, and cultural activities for nearly two centuries. The castle's structures feature a combination of Moorish, Indian, and local architectural elements, adorned with beautiful frescoes and intricate carvings. It also housed several religious buildings, including churches and mosques, showcasing Ethiopia's diverse religious history. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1979, Gondar Castle remains a testament to Ethiopia's glorious past, attracting visitors from around the world who come to admire its majestic architecture and immerse themselves in the country's compelling history.
